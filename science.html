01.

Amalie Emmy Noether

The name NoetherTech is an acknowledgement and homage to one of the greatest mathematicians in history, Amalie Emmy Noether. She has been praised as the most important woman in the history of mathematics by Albert Einstein, Jean Dieudonné, and Norbert Wiener, to name a few. After she began her work as a professor, Noether made a multitude of contributions over her 27 years of professional work across many mathematical fields. She was especially remembered for her work on abstract algebra, a highly innovative field of 20th-century mathematics, that is still deeply studied to this day.

In 1915, Noether was recruited to help solve an issue with Einstein's theory of general relativity. The resulting solution was named Noether's theorem, which states that conserved quantities can be found in symmetries in the laws of nature. Although this theory has become one of the fundamental tools in modern theoretical physics, it has also reshaped the way we approach many problems in the physical world. In particular, the inspiration behind much of the technology at NoetherTech can be traced back to, and credited to this particular piece of revolutionary work by Emmy Noether.

02.

The Science Explained

Laws of Thermodynamics

  1. Total energy of any isolated system is constant
  2. The entropy of any isolated system always increases
  3. Entropy of a system approaches a constant value as the temperature approaches absolute zero

NoetherTech's technology was developed based on the fundamental principles of physics in laws of symmetry and conservation, thermodynamics, information theory, and understanding of neural default states and their roles in neural physiology. Our EMBP treatment model is developed based on the theory that the brain can be modeled as an open thermodynamic system with dissipative structure. The priciple that guides the coordinated neuronal connection and behavior is the minimal entropy law described in dissipative thermodynamic theory. The massive synaptic connections effectively reduce the translational and rotational degrees of freedom of all neurons (vibrational degrees of freedom are negligible at body temperature), and the coherent neuronal firing resembles formation of translational symmetry. The former strategy decreases the total level of kinetic energy, and the latter reduces energy consumption through conservation to increase the stability in the brain as a whole system.

The brain, and many other biological systems, is an open thermodynamic system that takes matter and free energy from the environment to form order within the system and dissipate entropy outward to the environment. It is constrained by a fixed inflow of free energy in the form of chemical nourishment and information from the environment. The internal spontaneous process may be described as a steady state of entropy production.

Biological systems, including the brain, must follow the first principles of physics

A neural system, for example, will not violate the law to spontaneously increase its energy metabolism to risk its stability. Quite the opposite, the default state of the brain has been recently found to be in the lowest energy state with rhythmic electric oscillation. In certain pathological conditions, such as stroke and concussion, the brain would turn itself into even lower frequency activity to conserve energy regardless as to how much cognitive or sensory motor functions would be affected. In other words, our culturally defined brain functions are not necessarily in the best interest of the brain. Our biological brain function is primarily concerned with survival.

According to this model, cognitive functions can be described as epiphenomena of the brain’s strategies to maintain stability. Structure damage or change in symmetric neuronal firing alters the states of energy; levels of deviation from the norm may result in various changes in behaviors or other symptoms. In this limited sense, there are much fewer brain energy states tan phenomenological measures of symptoms. EMBP is designed to evaluated and normalize deviation of the brain state to help improve the slinical symptoms. The treatment protocol consists of two components: an electroencephalography (EEG_ recording to evaluate the brain state, and an individualized transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) treatment guided by the EEG to normalize that state.

Furthermore, the brain's oscillatory default state reveals obedience of another physical law - conservation in symmetry. Energy must be conserved in an oscillation invariant over time. The more symmetrical the EEG oscillation (greater frequency selectivity) is, the lower the brain metabolic rate it has. In addition, the frequency selectivity of EEG (low energy measure) has been found to be predictive for subject's cognitive performance. We thus speculate that culturally accepted cognitive functions are likely to be epiphenomena of the brain's survival strategy. Dealing with mental conditions must consider the brain's default "interests."
